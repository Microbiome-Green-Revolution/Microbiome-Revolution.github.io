Georgios Papaioannou is a Laboratory Technician on the Microbiome Green Revolution project, based at Silwood Park in the Waring lab. He contributes to Task 3, investigating the drivers of pathogen dynamics through environmental microbiology and field-based experimentation. His work includes soil and root sampling, environmental DNA extraction, qPCR assays, and surveillance of Gaeumannomyces tritici (take-all),  Georgios holds an MSc in Plant Genetics and Crop Improvement from the University of East Anglia and an integrated master's in Agricultural Science from the Agricultural University of Athens. Before joining Imperial, he researched disease resistance in soybean at 2Blades, applying GWAS and high-throughput genotyping to identify resistance loci.
